Notes

This demonstration is based on the ideas of A. Peres, J. Phys. A24, 174-178 (1991) and N.D. Mermin, Rev. Mod. Phys. 65, 803-815 (1993). The latter article mentions a number of references that also influenced this work.

The work of Peres and Mermin was further developed by A.Cabello, Phys.Rev.Lett. 87, 010403 (2001) and P.K.Aravind, Found. Phys. Lett. 15, 397 (2002).

The demonstration, as given here, closely parallels the account in P.K. Aravind, Am. J. Phys. 72, 1303-1307 (2004).

The Mermin-Peres Magic Square, as it is sometimes called, has been used to propose a type of quantum game known as Pseudo-Telepathy by Prof. Gilles Brassard and his collaborators at the Universite de Montreal. A number of physicists and computer scientists have contributed to the further development of this new offshoot of traditional game theory. For a brief survey, see: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_pseudo-telepathy

A laboratory realization of this demonstration has been achieved by Jia-Min Xu et.al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 050402 (2022). A non-technical account of their experiment, including a discussion of its limitations and its broader significance, can be found at: https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/researchers-use-quantum-telepathy-to-win-an-impossible-game
